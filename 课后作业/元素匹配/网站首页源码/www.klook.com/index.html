Singapore

Despite being a tiny island and a former fishing village, the modern city of Singapore is filled with so much to explore! Visit the iconic symbol of Singapore at Merlion Park and enjoy views of the waterfront before heading off to Gardens by the Bay where you can witness various flora in bloom. For the best views of the Singapore cityscape, head to the Marina Bay Sands.

If you're looking for adventure, spend a day at Sentosa Island and indulge in various outdoor activities or head to Universal Studios Singapore for some heart-pumping fun. You can even visit animals at the Singapore Zoo or the Night Safari, the world's first nocturnal zoo.

Sights you can't miss in Singapore

Sentosa

Sentosa is a must-visit tourist spot in Singapore. This island resort offers a wide range of attractions and activities that cater to all ages and interests. One of the main highlights is Universal Studios Singapore, a world-class theme park with thrilling rides and shows. Sentosa also boasts beautiful beaches, such as Siloso Beach and Palawan Beach, where visitors can relax and soak up the sun. Other attractions include the S.E.A. Aquarium, Adventure Cove Waterpark, and the iconic Merlion statue. Additionally, Sentosa is conveniently located near other popular attractions in Singapore, such as Gardens by the Bay and Marina Bay Sands, making it an ideal destination for tourists.

Gardens by the Bay Station

Gardens by the Bay Station is a must-visit tourist spot in Singapore. This iconic attraction is known for its stunning futuristic design and beautiful gardens. The highlight of the Gardens is the Supertree Grove, where towering tree-like structures are adorned with vertical gardens and illuminated at night, creating a magical atmosphere. The Flower Dome and Cloud Forest are two conservatories that showcase a wide variety of plants and flowers from around the world. Nearby attractions worth exploring include Marina Bay Sands, where you can enjoy panoramic views of the city from the SkyPark, and the Merlion Park, where you can take a photo with the iconic Merlion statue.

Saint John's Island

Saint John's Island is a must-visit tourist spot located in Singapore. This picturesque island offers a serene and tranquil escape from the bustling city life. The island is known for its pristine beaches, crystal-clear waters, and lush greenery, making it a perfect destination for nature lovers and beach enthusiasts. Visitors can indulge in various activities such as swimming, snorkeling, and picnicking while enjoying the breathtaking views. Additionally, the island is home to a rich marine ecosystem, making it a popular spot for marine enthusiasts and nature photographers. Nearby attractions worth exploring include Lazarus Island and Kusu Island, both of which offer stunning landscapes and cultural landmarks. Saint John's Island is definitely worth a visit for those seeking a peaceful and idyllic getaway.

Jewel Changi Airport

Jewel Changi Airport is a must-visit tourist spot in Singapore. It is not just an airport but also a world-class lifestyle destination. The main highlight of Jewel is the stunning indoor waterfall, known as the Rain Vortex, which is the tallest indoor waterfall in the world. Visitors can enjoy a mesmerizing light and sound show at night. The place also offers a wide range of shopping options, from luxury brands to local boutiques. Food lovers will be delighted with the diverse dining options available, including Michelin-starred restaurants. Nearby attractions worth exploring include the Singapore Botanic Gardens and the iconic Marina Bay Sands.

Little India,Singapore

Little India,Singapore is a vibrant and culturally rich neighborhood in Singapore that is definitely worth visiting. It offers a unique and immersive experience of Indian culture, with its colorful streets, bustling markets, and authentic Indian cuisine. The area is known for its beautiful Hindu temples, such as the Sri Veeramakaliamman Temple and the Sri Srinivasa Perumal Temple, which are must-visit attractions. Little India,Singapore is also a great place to shop for traditional Indian clothing, jewelry, and spices. Nearby attractions include the Arab Street, where you can explore the Arab Quarter and visit the stunning Sultan Mosque. Overall, Little India,Singapore is a must-visit destination for anyone looking to experience the vibrant Indian culture in Singapore.

Chinatown Singapore

Chinatown Singapore is a vibrant and culturally rich neighborhood that is definitely worth visiting. It offers a unique blend of Chinese heritage and modernity, making it a must-visit for tourists. The bustling streets are lined with traditional shophouses, where you can find a wide array of authentic Chinese cuisine, traditional Chinese medicine shops, and souvenir stores. The highlight of Chinatown is the Sri Mariamman Temple, the oldest Hindu temple in Singapore, which showcases stunning architecture and intricate sculptures. Nearby attractions include the Buddha Tooth Relic Temple, where you can learn about Buddhism and admire the impressive Buddha tooth relic, as well as the vibrant Maxwell Food Centre, known for its delicious local street food.

Merlion Park

The main highlight of this park is the iconic Merlion statue, a mythical creature with the head of a lion and the body of a fish. The statue spouts water from its mouth, creating a mesmerizing sight. Visitors can take memorable photos with the Merlion as a backdrop. The park also offers stunning views of the Marina Bay Sands and the Singapore skyline. Nearby attractions worth exploring include the Marina Bay Sands complex, Gardens by the Bay, and the ArtScience Museum. Merlion Park is a symbol of Singapore and a must-visit for any traveler.

Fort Canning Park

Fort Canning Park is a must-visit tourist spot in Singapore. This historic park offers a serene escape from the bustling city and is rich in cultural significance. The park is home to several historical landmarks, including the Fort Canning Hill, which served as a strategic military base during World War II. Visitors can explore the park's lush greenery, visit the archaeological dig site, and enjoy panoramic views of the city skyline. Additionally, Fort Canning Park hosts various events and concerts throughout the year, making it a vibrant and lively destination. Nearby attractions worth exploring include the National Museum of Singapore and the Singapore River.

Fountain of Wealth

The Fountain of Wealth is a must-visit attraction located in Singapore, Singapore. It holds the Guinness World Record for being the largest fountain in the world. This iconic landmark is not only visually stunning but also holds cultural significance as it symbolizes wealth and prosperity. Visitors can participate in a unique ritual by walking around the fountain three times for good luck. Surrounding the fountain is the Suntec City Mall, a popular shopping destination with a wide range of shops and restaurants. Additionally, nearby attractions include the Esplanade - Theatres on the Bay and the Marina Bay Sands, making it a perfect spot to explore Singapore's vibrant cityscape.

Mount Faber Park

Mount Faber Park is a must-visit tourist spot in Singapore. Situated in the heart of the city, it offers breathtaking panoramic views of the city skyline and the surrounding islands. The park is known for its lush greenery, well-maintained walking trails, and stunning viewpoints. Visitors can take a cable car ride to reach the park, adding an element of adventure to their visit. Additionally, there are several dining options available at the top, allowing visitors to enjoy a meal with a view. Nearby attractions worth exploring include the Henderson Waves, a pedestrian bridge with unique architecture, and the Southern Ridges, a scenic trail connecting several parks.

FAQs about Singapore

What is Singapore Best Known For?

Singapore is a city dweller’s dream. Its sustainable parks, futuristic architecture, green urban planning, and diverse cultural background sets a colorful backdrop for go-getters. Even with its fast-paced modernization and relentless ambition, this city-state is still rooted in its humble beginnings as evidenced by its rich hawker food culture.

When is the Best Time to Visit Singapore?

Summer is always the best time to visit a tropical country like Singapore, which runs from June-August. But if you’d like to partake in national celebrations, January-March are when the Lunar New Year, Ramadan, Holi, and National Day take place.

Where is the Best Location for Tourists to Stay in Singapore?

If you’re the type of tourist who indulges in luxurious stays, Marina Bay’s central location and gorgeous cityscape fit the bill. The hip and trendy Clarke Quay is just a stone’s throw away, an area known for its exciting nightlife. But if you’re keen on cultural exchange, Little India and Chinatown are great options because both offer good food, authenticity, and budget-friendly prices.
